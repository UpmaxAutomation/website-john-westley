## The Problem with How This Usually Gets Fixed

Before getting to the engagement, it's worth naming what doesn't work — because you've likely tried some of it.

The instinct at your stage is to change the people, add a methodology, or build more pipeline. These are execution fixes. They work when the problem is execution.

When the problem is structural — when the same symptoms appear regardless of who is in the role or which methodology is running — execution fixes don't hold. They redistribute the same structural problem onto new people.

**Structural problems require structural diagnosis and a sequenced fix.**

That's what this engagement delivers.

## Engagement Overview

### Three Phases

**Phase 1 — Revenue Journey Audit**
*Weeks 1–3*

A structured diagnostic across every stage of the revenue journey:
- How marketing generates and qualifies demand
- How leads move (or don't) from marketing to sales
- How qualified prospects convert (or stall)
- How new customers onboard and activate
- How customers retain, expand, and refer

**What I need access to:** Key leaders in sales, marketing, and CS (4–6 interviews); CRM data; any existing reporting on pipeline, retention, and expansion; current playbooks, if they exist.

**Deliverable:** Revenue Journey Map — a visual diagnostic of where revenue is being created, where it's leaking, and which structural patterns are active.

---

**Phase 2 — Structural Diagnosis**
*Weeks 3–5*

Root-cause analysis of each structural pattern identified in the audit. This goes beyond naming the problem — it explains:
- Why the pattern exists (usually: the motion was designed for an earlier version of the company)
- What it's costing in real revenue terms
- What fixing it requires across functions
- Which problems to fix first, second, and third — because order matters

**Deliverable:** Structural Diagnosis Report — a board-ready document with specific findings, sequenced interventions, and projected impact per fix.

---

**Phase 3 — Redesign & Rebuild**
*Weeks 5–16*

Implementation of the redesigned revenue motion. This is not a slide deck handoff — I stay in the room.

Depending on the specific structural problems identified, this phase typically includes:
- Redesigning the marketing-to-sales handoff (if applicable)
- Building or redesigning the conversion architecture
- Designing the onboarding and retention motion (if applicable)
- Building the expansion motion (if applicable)
- Aligning team incentives and metrics to the redesigned structure
- Establishing measurement and leading indicators for the new motion

**Deliverable:** A working revenue motion — redesigned processes, updated playbooks, aligned team metrics, and a measurement dashboard.
