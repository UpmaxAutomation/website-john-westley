## About Section (2,600 char max)

```
Most B2B revenue problems get diagnosed wrong.

The pipeline stalls. Customers don't expand. A good quarter doesn't repeat. The instinct is to change the people — fire the VP Sales, get better leads, push harder on the reps.

Sometimes that's right.

More often, the problem is structural. Not who's executing — but how the revenue motion was designed.

I've been in the room when it goes wrong. CRO. VP Sales. VP Marketing. CEO. For years, I owned the number — and kept finding the same thing across company after company, industry after industry: six structural patterns that silently tax every dollar of revenue, regardless of how hard the team works.

I call them the Six Structural Taxes. You've probably felt them even if you haven't named them:

→ A broken handoff between marketing and sales that kills qualified interest before it becomes pipeline
→ Customers arriving but not staying — acquisition working, retention draining
→ Growth that hits the same ceiling no matter how much you invest
→ An expansion motion that was never built — leaving your highest-margin revenue unrealized
→ Qualified prospects who go quiet — not because of your reps, but because the conversion architecture is missing
→ Teams working hard in parallel, none of it compounding

What I do: diagnose which of these are active in your business, sequence the interventions in the right order (fixing the wrong one first can make things worse), and stay through implementation until the new motion holds.

This isn't a framework I apply to every company. It's a diagnosis. Every engagement starts with an audit — because the right answer depends entirely on what's actually broken.

Results from structural fixes, not new hires or bigger marketing budgets:
— $6M to $84M — same team, redesigned revenue motion
— 117% Net Revenue Retention
— $3.5M recovered in six months from one structural fix
— 24% unit sales lift without a headcount increase

I work with B2B companies between $3M and $50M. Typically 2–3 engagements at a time. If you're in that range and revenue is working harder than it's growing — let's have one honest conversation.

Book at growththatholds.com or message me here.
```
