# John Westley — Target Audience & ICP Avatars

---

## ICP Overview

**Company profile:**
- B2B SaaS, services, or tech-enabled services
- Revenue: $3M–$50M ARR
- Team size: 15–200 employees
- Sales motion: 3–18 month enterprise/mid-market sales cycles
- Has invested in sales, marketing, and tools — but growth feels harder than it should

**Negative ICP (do not target):**
- Consumer businesses
- <$3M revenue (too early for structural diagnosis to matter at scale)
- >$50M revenue (structural problems exist but complexity requires a different engagement model)
- Companies where the founder is not yet ready to examine structural causes
- Companies looking for a new CRM, sales trainer, or marketing agency

## Avatar 1 — The Plateauing CEO

**Name:** Marcus, 44
**Title:** CEO
**Company:** B2B SaaS, $8M ARR, 45 employees, Series A
**Location:** Chicago / Austin / Denver

### Who He Is
Marcus built this company to $8M by selling himself and with a scrappy team. He brought in a VP of Sales 18 months ago. They're decent. Pipeline looks okay on paper. But somehow they keep hitting the same ARR range — some quarters up, some back down. He's not sure what's wrong, and that uncertainty bothers him more than the slow growth does.

### What Keeps Him Up at Night
- Revenue feels fragile, not compounding
- He's hired better people but the number keeps behaving the same way
- His board is asking questions he doesn't have clean answers to
- He's worried the VP Sales is the problem — but something tells him it might be something else
- He's tried three different sales methodologies in two years. None of them stuck.

### How He Thinks About the Problem
He frames it as a sales problem. Maybe a marketing problem. He's been pitched RevOps consulting, sales coaching, a new CRM, and more pipeline. He's done some of them. Nothing has fundamentally shifted the trajectory.

### What He Needs to Hear
That the problem might not be who's executing the revenue motion — but how the motion was designed. That diagnosis comes before prescription. That someone who's been in every seat at the table can see what he can't from any one seat.

### Where to Reach Him
- LinkedIn (active, follows revenue/SaaS content)
- SaaS founder communities (Pavilion, SaaStr, local founders circles)
- PE/VC-adjacent networks if has institutional investors

### Hook Lines for This Avatar
- "Your VP Sales isn't the problem. The motion they're selling into might be."
- "Revenue that keeps hitting the same ceiling usually has a structural ceiling — not a people problem."
- "Most $8M companies have at least two of the six structural taxes running. Do you know which ones?"

## Avatar 2 — The New CRO

**Name:** Rachel, 39
**Title:** Chief Revenue Officer
**Company:** B2B tech company, $15M ARR, just hired 3 months ago
**Location:** Remote (SaaS) or SF / NYC / Boston

### Who She Is
Rachel was brought in to fix a revenue motion that the previous VP Sales couldn't crack. She's sharp, credible, has done this before. But she's walking into a situation with existing team dynamics, inherited processes, and a CEO who's waiting to see results. She has 6–9 months to show meaningful change.

### What Keeps Her Up at Night
- She can feel that something structural is wrong but hasn't been able to name it precisely
- She needs to make changes that stick — not surface-level changes the org rejects
- She's worried about creating the wrong diagnosis and wasting 6 months fixing the wrong thing
- She has credibility to spend but doesn't want to spend it on the wrong bet
- The board and CEO think the problem is the team. She suspects it's the motion.

### How She Thinks About the Problem
She's more sophisticated than the CEO about revenue operations. She knows "hire better reps" isn't the answer. She's looking for a structured way to identify what's actually broken and fix it in the right order.

### What She Needs to Hear
That someone can come in alongside her — not to replace her judgment, but to give her a rigorous, cross-functional diagnostic framework she can bring to the CEO and board. That she can accelerate her own credibility by bringing in someone who's seen this pattern hundreds of times.

### Where to Reach Him
- LinkedIn (very active, follows RevOps/CRO content)
- Pavilion / RevGenius / CRO-specific Slack communities
- Referred by a PE firm or board member

### Hook Lines for This Avatar
- "The hardest thing about walking into a broken revenue motion: knowing which of the six things to fix first."
- "You can feel the structure is wrong before you can name it. That's where the diagnostic starts."
- "New CROs who diagnose the motion before changing the team win credibility fast. The ones who start with personnel changes often spend it faster."

## Avatar 3 — The PE-Backed Portfolio CEO

**Name:** David, 52
**Title:** CEO (PE-installed or PE-backed)
**Company:** B2B services or tech-enabled services, $12M–$35M revenue, backed 12–18 months
**Location:** Midwest, Southeast, or remote

### Who He Is
David runs a portfolio company for a mid-market PE firm. The thesis at acquisition was that revenue growth was the primary value-creation lever. Twelve months in, growth hasn't materialized the way the model projected. The operating partner is asking hard questions. The next board meeting is uncomfortable.

### What Keeps Him Up at Night
- He's accountable to a model that assumed faster revenue growth
- He's been told to "fix sales" but the operating partner hasn't given him a specific diagnosis
- He needs to show the board a credible plan, not just more of the same
- He doesn't know if the problem is the team, the market, the product, or the motion
- Time is compressing — he has a MOIC to hit

### How He Thinks About the Problem
David thinks operationally. He likes frameworks, timelines, deliverables. He wants a clear diagnosis with a sequenced plan and measurable outcomes. He's not looking for a philosophy lecture.

### What He Needs to Hear
A structured diagnostic process with clear deliverables and a revenue journey map he can present to his operating partner and board. Credibility from John's track record in PE-adjacent contexts. Speed — he needs answers in weeks, not months.

### Where to Reach Him
- PE firm introduction (highest conversion)
- Operating partner networks
- LinkedIn (professional, not very active — needs a warm intro)

### Hook Lines for This Avatar
- "Your operating partner says 'fix sales.' The question is: fix what, exactly, and in what order?"
- "PE boards are asking why the revenue model isn't hitting. Usually the answer is structural, not personnel."
- "Most revenue audits take 90 days and produce a deck. This one takes 5 weeks and produces a sequenced fix."

## Avatar 4 — The Founder Hitting the Transition Wall

**Name:** Sofia, 37
**Title:** Founder & CEO
**Company:** B2B SaaS or agency, $3M–$6M revenue, 5-year-old business
**Location:** Anywhere

### Who She Is
Sofia built her company through founder-led sales and exceptional product. She's at the point where the growth rate is slowing, she can't scale by selling herself, and the team she's hired to replace her selling hasn't replicated her success. The business feels stuck. She's not sure if she needs a new sales leader, better marketing, or something more fundamental.

### What Keeps Her Up at Night
- Growth that was predictable at $1M–$3M has stalled at $4–5M
- Every new sales hire underperforms what she could do herself
- She doesn't know if that's a hiring problem or a process problem
- She's resistant to spending more money on marketing when it's unclear the sales motion can convert it
- She's worried about raising a round before fixing the fundamentals

### How She Thinks About the Problem
Sofia often frames this as a "scale" problem. She knows the product works. She knows customers are happy (mostly). She thinks she needs to find a great VP Sales. John would reframe this: the problem isn't who sells — it's that the motion hasn't been designed to be sold by anyone other than her.

### What She Needs to Hear
That this transition is a structural problem, not a hiring problem. That the revenue motion needs to be designed to be repeatable before it can be scaled. That she can fix this without waiting for the perfect VP Sales hire.

### Where to Reach Her
- Founder communities (YC Alumni, SaaStr, Pavilion)
- LinkedIn posts about founder-led sales
- Direct outreach after seeing her share content about scaling/growth challenges

### Hook Lines for This Avatar
- "Founder-led sales stalls not because the founder runs out of time — but because the motion was never designed to be run by anyone else."
- "If every sales hire underperforms what you can do, the motion is wrong. Not the hires."
- "The transition from $3M to $10M breaks one specific thing: the motion. Here's what to fix."

## Avatar 5 — The Board-Pressured VP Sales

**Name:** Chris, 45
**Title:** VP of Sales
**Company:** B2B mid-market, $10M–$25M, working for a CEO who's questioning results
**Location:** Enterprise sales markets (NYC, SF, Chicago, Atlanta)

### Who He Is
Chris has been VP Sales for 18 months. He's a good sales leader — maybe not perfect, but competent. But revenue isn't where it should be, and the CEO is starting to question whether the problem is him. He knows some of it is the motion — bad lead quality, broken handoffs from marketing, CS not doing expansions — but he can't get the CEO to see it as a systems problem. He's about to get blamed for a structural problem.

### What Keeps Him Up at Night
- He's going to be fired for problems he didn't create
- He can't get marketing or CS to own their part of the motion
- The CEO sees pipeline and close rate — not the structural causes behind them
- He needs a third party to validate the diagnosis he already has
- He wants a plan to fix it before he loses his seat

### What He Needs to Hear
That there's a way to bring a neutral diagnostic voice into the conversation — someone who can tell the CEO what the VP Sales has been saying, but from a position of credibility that doesn't feel like self-defense.

### Where to Reach Him
- LinkedIn DMs with a reframe ("you probably already know what's wrong")
- Pavilion / revenue leadership communities
- Often referred by the CEO who is considering letting him go

### Hook Lines for This Avatar
- "You already know the structural problem. The question is whether the CEO is ready to hear it from someone other than you."
- "Before the CEO blames the VP Sales, it's worth asking: is the motion designed well enough for anyone to succeed in?"
