# John Westley — Referral Partner Brief

*A short guide for board members, PE operating partners, advisors, and trusted operators who want to refer John's work. Tells them who to refer, when to refer, and how to make the introduction.*

---

## What John Does (in one sentence)

John Westley diagnoses the structural reasons B2B revenue stalls — and redesigns the motion until growth compounds.

---

## Who to Refer

The best referrals are people in one of these five situations:

### 1. The CEO Whose Revenue Keeps Hitting the Same Ceiling
- Revenue in the $5M–$30M range
- ARR has been relatively flat for 2+ quarters
- They've tried things — new hire, new methodology, more pipeline — and nothing has fundamentally shifted
- They're beginning to wonder if the problem isn't the people

**The trigger phrase:** *"We keep hitting the same number."*

---

### 2. The CEO About to Make a Senior Revenue Hire
- About to hire a VP Sales, CRO, or CMO
- Company is at $5M–$25M
- The previous person in that seat didn't work out
- They're assuming the next hire will fix the problem

**The trigger phrase:** *"We need to find the right [revenue leader]."*

**Why this is a great referral moment:** If the motion is broken, the new hire will fail for the same structural reasons. A structural audit before the hire tells them what kind of leader they actually need — and what motion that person will be walking into.

---

### 3. The New CRO Walking into an Inherited Motion
- Just started (within first 90 days) or about to start
- Inherited a revenue team and a motion they didn't design
- Smart, credible, knows something structural is wrong but can't name it precisely
- Has 6–9 months to show the board meaningful progress

**The trigger phrase:** *"I'm trying to figure out what's actually broken before I make any big moves."*

---

### 4. The PE-Backed CEO Facing Board Pressure on Revenue
- PE-backed company, $10M–$40M
- Operating partner or board has told them to "fix sales"
- CEO doesn't have a specific enough diagnosis to act on
- MOIC is compressing

**The trigger phrase:** *"The board keeps asking about revenue and I don't have a clean answer yet."*

---

### 5. The VP Sales Being Blamed for a Structural Problem
- VP Sales has been in seat 12–24 months
- Revenue isn't where the CEO wants it
- CEO is considering replacing them
- VP Sales suspects the problem is the motion, not themselves, but can't get the CEO to hear it

**The trigger phrase:** *"I keep telling [CEO] the problem isn't my team — it's how the whole thing is set up."*

---

## Who NOT to Refer

- Companies below $3M revenue — too early for structural diagnosis to matter at scale
- Companies above $50M — structural problems exist but complexity requires a different engagement model
- Consumer businesses
- Companies looking for a sales trainer, coach, or marketing agency
- Founders who are not yet willing to examine the motion they built (this is a real thing — the referral will produce a frustrating call for everyone)

## What Happens After the Introduction

1. John reaches out within 24 hours
2. Books a 45-minute diagnostic conversation
3. Runs the diagnostic — no pitch, specific questions across their revenue journey
4. At the end: tells them honestly whether they have a structural problem and what it is

**Your referral will not be pitched to.** The call is genuinely diagnostic.
