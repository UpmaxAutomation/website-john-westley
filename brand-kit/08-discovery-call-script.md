# John Westley — Discovery Call Script

*45-minute diagnostic conversation. No pitch. No prep required from the prospect.*

---

## Pre-Call Prep (5 minutes before)

Before the call, quickly review:
- Their LinkedIn profile — title, tenure, company stage
- Company website — what they sell, revenue signals, team size
- Any prior context — how they found you, what they said in the booking form
- Their avatar type (Plateauing CEO / New CRO / PE-Backed / Founder / VP Sales)

**One question to hold in your head going in:**
> "What is the one structural thing this company is most likely to have broken at their stage?"

---

## Call Structure Overview

| Phase | Time | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Open | 0–3 min | Establish tone — diagnostic, not sales |
| Company context | 3–10 min | Size, stage, revenue motion basics |
| Revenue journey | 10–25 min | Where revenue is leaking — listen for structural signals |
| Pain sharpening | 25–35 min | Name what you're hearing, test resonance |
| Next step | 35–42 min | Qualify fit, offer path forward |
| Close | 42–45 min | Confirm next step |

---

## Opening (0–3 min)

**Set the frame immediately — before they can ask "so what do you do?"**

```
"Thanks for making the time. I want to use this as a genuine diagnostic — not a pitch. 

My job in the next 45 minutes is to ask questions that help us both understand whether you have a structural revenue problem, and if so, roughly where it lives. 

At the end, I'll tell you honestly what I'm hearing and whether I think there's something worth exploring together. Sound good?"
```

**Why this works:** Removes sales pressure, positions you as the expert running the diagnostic, not the vendor making the case.

---

## Company Context (3–10 min)

Get oriented. Keep it brisk — this is setup, not the substance.

```
"Before I ask about the revenue motion specifically — give me 60 seconds on where the company is right now. Revenue range, how long you've been at this stage, and how you'd describe the current state of growth."
```

Listen for:
- Revenue range (confirms ICP fit)
- How long they've been "here" — a year at the same number is a structural signal
- Tone — frustrated? Confused? Resigned? That's information.

**Follow-up if needed:**
```
"And the team — how many people own revenue directly? Sales, marketing, CS?"
```

---

## Revenue Journey Diagnostic (10–25 min)

This is the core. Work through the journey from acquisition to expansion. You're looking for where the structural gaps are — not validating what they think is wrong.

**Do not** accept their framing at face value. Their theory of the problem is usually wrong (or incomplete). Your job is to find the structural cause underneath their symptom description.

---

### Section A — Acquisition & Handoff

```
"Walk me through how a new customer finds you and gets to a first conversation. Where does top-of-funnel come from?"
```

Listen for: dependency on founder, single-channel fragility, undesigned inbound motion

```
"When a lead comes in — what happens in the first 24–48 hours? Who owns it, what's the sequence, how does it get from marketing to sales?"
```

**Structural signal:** Hesitation here, "it depends," or "we're still figuring that out" = broken handoff

```
"When a qualified lead goes cold — where in that sequence does it usually happen?"
```

---

### Section B — Conversion

```
"Take me through a deal that should have closed but didn't. Not a bad-fit lead — a real prospect who went quiet or chose not to move forward. What happened?"
```

Listen for: consistency — if multiple deals stall at the same stage, that's structural

```
"Is there a stage in the sales cycle where you consistently lose momentum? Same objection, same type of buyer, same point in the process?"
```

**Structural signal:** "Yes, it usually happens after the demo / after legal / after we send pricing" = conversion architecture gap

```
"When a deal closes, what did it take? What was the moment it became real?"
```

---

### Section C — Onboarding & Retention

```
"What happens in the first 90 days after a customer signs? Walk me through it as a sequence — not the goal, but what actually happens."
```

Listen for: ad hoc onboarding, no designed activation, CS as reactive support not proactive motion

```
"What's your current net revenue retention? And how do you feel about that number — is it where it should be?"
```

**Structural signal:** NRR below 100% almost always = broken onboarding or missing retention motion

```
"When customers leave — what's the reason they give? And what do you think the real reason is?"
```

---

### Section D — Expansion

```
"How does expansion revenue happen in your business? Walk me through how a customer goes from one product or tier to a larger relationship."
```

Listen for: "it's mostly inbound from the customer asking" or "our AEs handle it when they notice it" = no designed expansion motion

```
"Do you have a specific motion — a trigger, a sequence, a team that owns it — or does it happen more organically?"
```

**Structural signal:** "Organically" = the highest-margin revenue in the business is being left on the table

```
"What percentage of your ARR growth last year came from expansion vs. new logo? And is that split where you want it?"
```

---

### Section E — Team & Alignment

```
"If I talked to your head of marketing and your head of sales separately and asked them to describe the ideal customer and the handoff process — would I get the same answer?"
```

(Watch the reaction — a pause or a laugh tells you more than the answer)

```
"Are there metrics one team is optimizing for that create friction for another team downstream?"
```

Listen for: classic examples — marketing optimizing for MQL volume, sales optimizing for close rate, CS optimizing for CSAT, none of them optimizing for NRR or expansion

---

## Pain Sharpening (25–35 min)

You've been listening. Now name what you heard. This is where the call turns.

**Reflect back what you heard — don't ask if it's right, state it:**

```
"Let me tell you what I'm hearing — tell me if I'm getting this wrong.

[Summarize 2–3 specific structural signals you observed]

The pattern you're describing — [their specific situation] — is one I've seen before. 

It's not [their theory of the problem — e.g., 'a rep performance issue' or 'a lead quality issue']. It's structural. Specifically: [name the structural tax you believe is active].

What happens with this kind of problem is [explain the structural dynamic in plain terms]. And the reason it persists is [why it's invisible from their seat].

Does that match what you're experiencing?"
```

**Why this works:** You're demonstrating diagnostic capability, not selling. When you name the problem more precisely than they did, credibility compounds.

---

**If they confirm:**
```
"What have you tried so far to address it?"
```

Listen for: the usual list (new hire, new tool, new methodology). Validate that those are execution fixes for a structural problem — they won't hold.

**If they're not sure:**
```
"Here's what I'd want to do. The next step isn't a proposal — it's me spending two weeks looking at the full journey with your team. What I've described is my working hypothesis from a 45-minute conversation. A proper diagnosis would either confirm it or find something different. Either way, you'd have an answer you don't have now."
```

---

## Fit Qualification (35–42 min)

Before offering a path forward, confirm fit:

**Revenue range:** (should already know, but confirm if unclear)
```
"Just to orient the scope — are you comfortable sharing where revenue is right now? Ballpark is fine."
```

**Decision-making:**
```
"If this diagnostic conversation pointed to a clear structural problem, who would be involved in a decision to do something about it? Just you, or would others need to be in the room?"
```

**Urgency:**
```
"What's the timeline pressure on this? Is this a 'something needs to change in 90 days' situation, or more of a 'we're planning for next year' conversation?"
```

**Openness (the critical question):**
```
"One thing I've learned: structural fixes require looking at how things work across functions — not just one team. Is that kind of cross-functional view something leadership is open to right now?"
```

**If the answer is yes to all:** High-fit, proceed to close.
**If decision-making is complex:** Ask to bring the right person into a second conversation.
**If no urgency:** Nurture — send case study relevant to their situation, check back in 60 days.
**If not cross-functionally open:** Not the right moment — be honest about it.

---

## Close — Next Step (42–45 min)

**If qualified and interested:**
```
"Here's what I'd suggest. The 45-minute conversation gave me a working hypothesis. To give you a real diagnosis — not a guess — I'd want to spend a week looking at the full motion with your team. 

The output is a Revenue Journey Map: a specific picture of where revenue is being created, where it's leaking, and what structural problems are active. That gives you something concrete to act on — or to bring to your board.

The way to start is simple: I'll send you a one-page scope of what a Revenue Journey Audit covers, what I'd need access to, and what the output looks like. Take a look, and if it makes sense, we pick a start date.

Does that seem like a reasonable next step?"
```

**If interested but timing is uncertain:**
```
"I'd rather you come to this when the timing is right than rush into it. Let me send you a short doc on what a structural diagnosis actually looks like — the process, the deliverables, the timeline. If the timing shifts, you'll have everything you need to move quickly."
```

**Always send within 24 hours:**
- One-page scope of Revenue Journey Audit
- Relevant case study (match to their situation)
- Link to the relevant section of the website

## Signals That Indicate Structural Problems

Use this list to recognize patterns during the call:

| What they say | Structural signal |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "We keep hitting the same ARR ceiling" | Invisible growth ceiling |
| "Leads go cold after the demo" | Conversion architecture gap |
| "Marketing and sales don't agree on lead quality" | Broken handoff |
| "Our reps try hard but close rates are inconsistent" | Conversion architecture gap |
| "Customers are happy but don't expand" | Missing expansion motion |
| "We lost two customers we shouldn't have" | Retention drain / broken onboarding |
| "We've tried X methodology and it worked for a while then stopped" | Structural problem, not methodology |
| "The team is working really hard" + slow growth | Structural misalignment |
| "It depends on which rep / which segment" | No designed motion, personality-dependent |
| "We grew well until [stage/hire/event]" | Motion designed for an earlier version of the company |

---

## What Not to Do

- Don't interrupt their story with your solution — even when you can see the answer
- Don't accept their framing ("our VP Sales isn't working out") without probing the structure beneath it
- Don't pitch the engagement before you've demonstrated diagnostic capability
- Don't skip the expansion section — that's where the most recoverable revenue usually hides
- Don't let the call run over 50 minutes — it signals you couldn't control the diagnostic
- Don't end without a specific next step agreed on the call
